On the poverty of student life. 2016. Reissue of the pamphlet 'On the poverty of student life' written by Tunisian Situationist Mustapha/Omar Khayati. It was published 50 years ago by five students at the University of Strasbourg. It caused significant uproar and precipitated the events of May 1968 in France. This dry needle copperetching is almost an exact copy of the version published by Black & Red in Detroit 2000. Yet the handmade engravings make it look like an old document and rather presented on a wall than in book form, it counters the activist writing. It becomes more image than text.
One must not imitate life, one must work like it. 2016. After a school trip to Amsterdam to visit several artists in their atelier, the cost of the bus turned out to be more then expected. The teachers suggested we all made a work inspired on our trip to sell for 10 euros to cover these costs. I thought it was a bit weird we had to sell work so I decided to bake five loafs of bread. A loaf usualy costs close to 2 euros so the value of 10 euros was already present. Based on Job Koelewijns use of taoist proverbs I flowered the second part of a chinese proverb on the bread, the first part printed on a note. The proverb 'One must not imitate life, one must work like it.' was once said by Picasso to Malraux as the greatest thing ever said about painting.
